​​​​​​​Too often, as a solopreneur or a shopkeeper, your brand may be mere afterthoughts and honestly, I can't blame you. There are thousands of tasks each day that you must attend to. 

Maybe you're here because you're looking for a quick-and-easy fix. You may be thinking, "I need a logo." I'm sorry to spoil your day, but a "logo" is not your brand. I would go so far as to say that, as a small business, you will be better off without a silly-looking "logo" anyone can whip up on a whim (or with a so-called "free online logo generator," ick).

There is an overload of information online when it comes to branding your small business. There are too many self-styled "online marketing experts" and "business gurus" offering misleading and self-contradictory advice to get you to buy their ware. Unfortunately, almost everything you read about branding is obsolete, misleading, or plain wrong. 

ADSTELLAM believes that a brand is an outward expression of your inward values. As a national flag is a crystallization of a country's culture, history, values, and aspirations, the brand symbolizes your "Big Why" as well as being a touchstone of specific customer experiences. 
Imaginative and intelligent, ADSTELLAM sees the big picture behind your brand and yet obsessively detail-oriented. 

ADSTELLAM's process-oriented approach to brand development begins and ends with the exploration of your core values and aspiration for the highest degree of ethical and social consciousness. Amidst the sea of ersatz consumer brands and their mindless imitators, ADSTELLAM raises the standard of excellence in small business and personal branding. 

With ADSTELLAM, you will get more than most creative agencies or business consultants normally offer (at a small fraction of the price they charge); you will discover your soul and learn how to package your soul's message to the world through your brand.

About ADSTELLAM​​​​​​​

Formerly Limeadestand Works, ADSTELLAM was founded in 2016 as technical assistance and educational service for aspiring and new entrepreneurs. The seeds of what is today's ADSTELLAM, however, were sown in the spring of 2011 as part of a community organizing project at Dorothy Day Community School of Sisters Of The Road in Portland, Oregon. 

Originally conceived as a project to build community resiliency among the low-income and houseless people through solidarity economy and microenterprise, the idea was independently revived in 2015 as a practical alternative to anti-poverty activism.

Over time, Limeadestand Works have branched out to too many side projects and lost its brand focus and cohesion. In July 2019, Limeadestand Works began a process of refining its brand. This journey ultimately took Limeadestand Works to become ADSTELLAM, now focusing solely on brand development.

ADSTELLAM is based in the majestic Pacific Northwest, on the ancestral land of the Tsinuk, Stl'pulmsh, Clatskanie, and Atfalati nations, better known as the metropolitan Portland region. 
​​​​​​​
Whether you are an aspiring solopreneur, a start-up micro-business, a community organization, or even an individual looking to reinvent yourself, ADSTELLAM is here for you. 

Willow

I'm a 6w5 and INTP.

As long as I can remember, I've been creating freedom with my own hands and brain. I've never been a popular kid or an overachiever. Rather, I have always been an outcast, a marginalized outsider looking in, a stranger in a strange land. (My childhood nickname was "E.T.") I grew up with a strong DIY ethos as a matter of survival. To keep my sanity, I've published my own newspapers and even had a tiny underground radio station--years before I discovered the Internet.

Since the early days of my life, I've always been a keen observer of media, advertising, propaganda, and brands. In my adolescence, I developed an interest in graphic design. I studied fine art and journalism, making me an adept at pictures and words alike. 

I survived at the intersections of challenging life circumstances and developed a keen sense of justice and solidarity with the underdogs, misunderstood, and forgotten. I learned to smell bullshit from ten miles away. 

Having to create opportunities for myself where none existed, I became not only an entrepreneur but also I live every aspect of my life with an entrepreneurial mindset. I have also learned how powerful it is to master the perceptions and words because oppressions and injustice are often perpetuated and exacerbated by certain forms of social conditioning.  

I am also a theologian, minister, and interfaith spiritual direction consultant (more about this on my other project, Willow Loves Thexlogy!). As a neurodiverse queer PoC, I particularly welcome minority and non-mainstream people to reach out to me.
